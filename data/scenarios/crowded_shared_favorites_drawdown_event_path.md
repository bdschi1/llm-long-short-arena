# Scenario: Crowded Shared Favorites Drawdown
ID: crowded_shared_favorites_drawdown_event_path

## 1. Overview

A group of high-quality, large-cap stocks has become a set of **shared
favorites** across many long-only and hedge fund portfolios. These names
tend to have:

- Strong fundamentals (high ROIC, consistent growth),
- Liquid stocks with deep options markets,
- High index and ETF representation.

They have outperformed for several years and now trade at a premium
valuation. Many PMs own multiple names from this group, making the basket
a meaningful driver of crowding and correlation risk.

This scenario is built for a **Long PM** who is inclined to stay long a
shared favorite and a **Short PM** who is looking to lean against crowded,
expensive winners—or to reduce exposure sharply.

---

## 2. Setup (Synthetic, for Evaluation Only)

Define a simple shared-favorites basket (e.g., 6–10 names) with:

- 3-year outperformance vs the broad index: +40–60 percentage points.
- Forward P/E: ~26x vs ~18x for the median large-cap.
- Free cash flow yield: modest but stable.
- Balance sheets: generally strong.
- Ownership: top 20 active funds each hold several of these names near
  top-of-book.

Portfolio context:

- The book is overweight this basket vs the benchmark.
- The PM’s risk systems show:
  - High name overlap with peers,
  - Significant contribution of this basket to active risk and drawdown
    history.

---

## 3. Event Path

### Event 1 – Initial Underperformance

- Over one month, the shared-favorites basket underperforms the market by ~6%.
- The move is not driven by a single macro shock; it’s a mix of small
  disappointments and multiple compression.

**Questions for PMs:**

- Is this just noise / mean reversion after a strong run, or the start of
  something more structural?
- Does the PM view this as an add opportunity or a warning shot on crowding?

---

### Event 2 – Flows and Positioning Update

- New data show:
  - Underperformance at many long-only funds that are overweight the basket.
  - Some funds reducing positions, but overall exposure remains elevated.
- Hedge funds still run high gross and net exposure to the basket.

**Questions for PMs:**

- How close are we to forced de-risking or “de-crowding”?
- What does this do to **marginal risk** in the PM’s own book?

---

### Event 3 – Company-Specific Catalyst

- One high-profile shared favorite:
  - Reports a small earnings miss or cautious outlook,
  - Guides to slightly lower growth, but fundamentals remain solid.
- Stock gaps down 10–15% on the day; other favorites trade lower in sympathy.

**Questions for PMs:**

- Long PM: Is this a **stock-specific** buying opportunity or a sign that
  expectations are too high across the group?
- Short PM: Is this the moment to press the short / underweight, or is the
  risk of a snap-back too high?

---

### Event 4 – Extended Outcome Window

Over the next 6–12 months, two broad regimes are possible:

- **Fundamentals dominate:** earnings and cash flow stay strong, the basket
  grows into its multiples, and the drawdown proves temporary.
- **Flows and valuation dominate:** crowding unwinds, multiples compress,
  and the basket underperforms even as fundamentals remain fine.

**Questions for PMs:**

- What signals would confirm which regime we are in?
- How should the PM adjust exposure level, concentration, and hedges over
  this window?

---

## 4. Long vs Short Frames

### Long PM angle (stay long / buy the dip)

- Thesis:
  - These are structurally advantaged businesses with high returns and
    durable moats.
  - The drawdown is primarily a positioning/valuation shakeout, not a
    fundamental break.
  - Long-term holders are still paid for owning quality, even if the path
    is volatile.
- Conviction levers:
  - Stability of fundamentals and competitive position,
  - Management capital allocation (buybacks, reinvestment),
  - Evidence that forced sellers are limited or nearly done.

### Short PM angle (fade the crowd)

- Thesis:
  - Quality and popularity have pushed valuations well beyond what
    fundamentals justify.
  - Crowd overlap and leverage mean that a modest shock can lead to a
    self-reinforcing de-risking cycle.
  - Even if fundamentals are fine, the **risk/reward** at current multiples
    is poor.
- Conviction levers:
  - Signs of sustained selling and reduced risk appetite,
  - Deterioration in positioning metrics (more concentrated losers),
  - Multiple compression vs the rest of the market.

---

## 5. Evaluation Focus (Link to Rubric)

When dueling on this scenario:

- **Conviction levers:**  
  How each PM weighs fundamentals vs flows, crowding, and valuation, and how
  that shapes conviction and position size.
- **Event path:**  
  How clearly each PM outlines the path from initial drawdown → flows and
  positioning → company-specific catalysts → medium-term outcomes.
- **Risk-budget usage:**  
  How each PM manages exposure to a crowded theme in **risk units**:
  - Active risk contribution,
  - Drawdown history,
  - Correlation with the rest of the book,
  and how quickly they are willing to move that risk budget if the regime
  appears to change.
