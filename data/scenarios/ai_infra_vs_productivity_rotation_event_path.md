# Scenario: AI Infrastructure vs Productivity Rotation
ID: ai_infra_vs_productivity_rotation_event_path

## 1. Overview

There are two related trades in the book:

- **AI infrastructure leaders** – chip and cloud-infrastructure vendors that
  supply the compute and networking for AI workloads.
- **AI productivity beneficiaries** – companies in labor-intensive industries
  (e.g., IT services, business process outsourcing, clinical research,
  customer support platforms) that can expand margins and growth by
  deploying AI to automate workflows.

Over the last 18–24 months, AI infrastructure stocks have dramatically
outperformed, with high valuations and heavy positioning. Productivity
beneficiaries have begun to catch a bid but remain less crowded and more
controversial.

This scenario sets up a **Long PM** who prefers the productivity trade
and a **Short PM** who prefers to be short or underweight the
infrastructure leaders, with the same underlying question:

> How and when does leadership rotate from infra to productivity,
> and how should conviction and risk budget move along that path?

---

## 2. Setup (Synthetic, for Evaluation Only)

- **Infra basket proxy (INFRA)**  
  - 24-month performance: +150%  
  - Forward P/E: ~38x  
  - Revenue growth: +35–40% YoY, margins expanding  
  - Widely owned across long-only and hedge funds

- **Productivity basket proxy (PROD)**  
  - 24-month performance: +25%  
  - Forward P/E: ~20x  
  - Revenue growth: +8–12% YoY, margins starting to inflect as AI tools roll out  
  - Under-owned, mixed opinions on durability of AI impact

Book context:

- The portfolio already has:
  - Significant growth/tech factor exposure,
  - Some legacy infra holdings,
  - Limited direct exposure to productivity names.

## 3. Event Path

### Event 1 – Big Infra Earnings Print

- A major infra leader reports:
  - Strong current-quarter results,
  - Slightly more cautious commentary on the pace of new AI build-outs,
  - Margins still high but guide to “normalization over time.”
- Price reaction:
  - Stock trades flat to slightly down after a large run into earnings.

**Questions for PMs:**

- Is this “as good as it gets” or just a pause?
- Does the risk/reward skew for infra change, even if the thesis still holds?

---

### Event 2 – Productivity Commentary from Corporates

- A wide set of companies in labor-intensive sectors start:
  - Quantifying AI-driven efficiency gains (headcount savings, throughput),
  - Pointing to early revenue uplift from AI-enhanced offerings.
- A few productivity names raise guidance on the back of AI projects.

**Questions for PMs:**

- Are these anecdotes or the start of a durable margin and growth inflection?
- How does this shift conviction in PROD vs INFRA, and how should risk be reallocated?

---

### Event 3 – Positioning and Flow Signals

- New data on ownership and flows show:
  - Infra names heavily owned, with high concentration across many funds.
  - Productivity names still relatively under-owned.
- Volatility in infra names picks up after small disappointments.

**Questions for PMs:**

- How much of the risk budget is tied up in a crowded, high-vol trade?
- Does the PM cap infra risk and start building a more diversified productivity sleeve?

---

### Event 4 – Relative Performance Regime

- Over the next 6–12 months:
  - Either INFRA continues to outperform strongly,
  - Or productivity names begin to outperform as AI benefits show up in EPS.

**Questions for PMs:**

- How does the PM treat early relative-performance signals?
- At what point (both in P&L and in risk units) does the PM declare a regime
  shift and materially tilt the book?

---

## 4. Long vs Short Frames

### Long PM angle (pro-Productivity, cautious on Infra)

- Thesis:
  - AI productivity gains in services-heavy industries are underappreciated.
  - Adoption is slower to see, but once margin and FCF inflect, re-rating can be powerful.
  - Infra is a great business but crowded and closer to peak expectations.
- Conviction levers:
  - Concrete margin and efficiency improvements in PROD names.
  - Increasing guidance and disclosure on AI projects.
  - Signals of infra demand normalization and growing investor crowding.

### Short PM angle (pro-Infra, skeptical on Productivity)

- Thesis:
  - Infra remains the primary economic winner of AI; demand is more durable
    than skeptics expect.
  - Productivity stories are noisy, hard to measure, and vulnerable to
    disappointment and accounting noise.
  - Valuation premium for infra is justified; productivity re-rating will be uneven.
- Conviction levers:
  - Continued strong infra orders and backlog,
  - Limited real-world EPS impact from AI initiatives at PROD names,
  - Lack of broad multiple expansion in productivity beneficiaries.

## 5. Evaluation Focus (Link to Rubric)

When dueling on this scenario:

- **Conviction levers:**  
  Infra demand signals vs productivity margin/EPS evidence, positioning risk,
  and valuation spreads.
- **Event path:**  
  How each PM lays out the rotation path and updates conviction and sizing at
  each event node (earnings, commentary, flows, relative performance).
- **Risk-budget usage:**  
  How each PM sizes and caps exposure to a single theme across the book in
  **risk units** (volatility, factor contributions, drawdown impact), not
  just notional dollars.
